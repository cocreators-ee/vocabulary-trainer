Borrowing from a
Sami

language (compare
Southern Sami
gaasoe
(
“
cold mist
”
)

Proto-Samic
*kāsō

, from
Proto-Uralic
*käsä
(
“
dew
”
)

1

Originally a dialect word meaning 'mist, fog', adopted as an equivalent of
gas

by by Finnish physician and author Samuel Roos in 1845, by phonetic similarity with
Dutch
gas

(whence words in many other European languages, such as
English
gas

).
