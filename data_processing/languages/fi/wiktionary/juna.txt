Finnish
juna
Etymology

From a (Savonian) dialectal word meaning "line, queue". The modern sense "train" is a literary coinage (by an unknown author) from 1864. The dialectal word is usually considered an irregular, probably sound-symbolic, derivative of
juoni

. Among the dialectal variants (mostly sporadic) that can be considered are
jona

,
joni

,
jono

and
juno

. One explanation for the
-u-

may be influence from
juntu

.
