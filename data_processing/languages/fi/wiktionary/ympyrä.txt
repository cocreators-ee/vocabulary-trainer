Finnish
Etymology

Coined by Finnish physician and translator Wolmar Schildt in 1835 as a shortening (perhaps back-formation) of the dialectal terms
ympyriäinen

,
ymmyrkäinen
(
“
round
”
)

, ultimately from
ympäri

. The
-ä-

in
ympäri-

has turned into an
-y-

in these derivatives and thus in
ympyrä

as well, probably by influence from the preceding sounds (labial
ymm-

,
ymp-

). Arguably analyzable as
ymp-

+‎
-yrä

.
