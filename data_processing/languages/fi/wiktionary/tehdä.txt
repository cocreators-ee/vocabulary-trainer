Usage notes
(
to make/render
)
:
Which of the two options is used depends on the part of speech of the result. A noun is very often always expressed with the elative + accusative construction, while an adjective is primarily expressed with accusative + translative, although elative + accusative is sometimes used as well (especially when talking about personal perception or when the action does not render something into a result, but rather a consequence; see the usage examples above).
(
"do a", "pull a"; copy, emulate
)
:
What or who is being emulated is practically always in the plural, even if plural forms would not normally be used for that particular word (such as with country names).
