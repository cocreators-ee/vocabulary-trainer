Etymology

Formed with the suffix
-nko

from a root of unknown origin, which has been proposed, among others, to derive from
auer

(thus from earlier
*auderinko

),
[
1
]

or from a
Baltic

language (compare
Lithuanian
aušrà
(
“
dawn
”
)

). Alternatively originally possibly a compound, in which the latter component is related to
rinki
(
“
ring
”
)

, Swedish
ring

, while the initial component could be
auvo

.
[
2
]

Originally an innovation of the western dialects of Finnish (replacing
päivä

). Attested in written sources since the 1500s.
