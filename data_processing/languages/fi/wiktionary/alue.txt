Etymology

From
Proto-Finnic
*alovëh

. Equivalent to
ala

+‎
-ue

. First attested in 1829 in an edition of the newspaper Oulun Wiikko-Sanomia. According to SSA, the form
alue

may have been adopted in the standard language as a result of Karelian influence (
Karelian
aloveh

), being native only in the 'easternmost' of dialects;
[
1
]

the Tavastian dialectal word
aloo

is probably related, however.
