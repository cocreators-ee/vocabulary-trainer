Usage notes

The difference between the Finnish verbs
palaa

and
polttaa

is that with
palaa

, the fuel (or whatever is burning) is the subject of the sentence, while with
polttaa

, the fuel is an object (and who causes it to burn is the subject). Both can be translated as "to burn" into English.
