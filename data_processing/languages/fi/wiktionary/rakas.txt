Etymology

From
Proto-Finnic
*rak'as

, cognate with
Karelian
rakas
(
“
willing, eager, kind, dear
”
)

. Traditionally held to derive from
Proto-Finno-Ugric
*rakka
(
“
near; to approach
”
)

(compare
Hungarian
rokon
(
“
relative
”
)

), but more recently proposed to be borrowed from
Proto-Germanic
*frakaz
(
“
greedy, lustful
”
)

; the former theory suffers from a lack of cognates in other languages.
