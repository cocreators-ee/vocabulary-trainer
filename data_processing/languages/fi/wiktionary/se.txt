Usage notes
Both
tuo
and
se
can be translated as "that"; see
tuo
for more information on the difference between the two.
In colloquial and dialectal Finnish,
se
is the usual and neutral personal pronoun in the third person singular, and its standard Finnish counterpart
hän
is restricted to certain particular uses. Using
se
of a person carries no negative connotation.
Due to the influence of Germanic languages, and nowadays especially to that of English,
se
may often be used as a kind of definite article in colloquial Finnish, though in standard Finnish, where word order expresses whether something is definite or indefinite, this colloquial usage is ungrammatical. (Compare the usage of
yksi
.)
